ðŸ““ 4.5.0.1 Independent Capstone Project Overview

Congratulations! You've made it to the final section of your final in-class course at Fidgetech!

Overview of Sectionâ€‹

You will spend this section working independently on a cumulative capstone project of your choosing. Once you finish your capstone you will present it to the class. Later you'll also have the opportunity to present this project (or another you've completed 100% independently) to employers at an Epicodus Demo Day.

This lesson will outline the requirements and expectations for this section.

Requirementsâ€‹

  • Attendance policy still applies. You are still expected to attend Epicodus during normal class hours.

  • Your project must be completed independently. This is likely the project you'll be presenting at Demo Day. Employers attending this event expect to see examples of independent student work. While you may discuss, brainstorm, beta test, and advise one another, all code must be written by you, and you alone.

  • You must put in a minimun of 30 hours work. If you finish you MVP in less time that means your MVP was not ambitious enough.

Presentationsâ€‹

Once you've finished the project and put in the required 30+ hours of work you'll schedule a time with the instructors to present your project to the class. Take advantage of this opportunity to practice your pitch for Demo Day, and receive feedback on your MVP.

Plan to spend several minutes addressing the following:

  • Project Name and Objective: Why did you choose this project? What purpose does it fulfill?

  • Demonstration: Show and describe the features of your work. Use technical terms and try hard to make this a serious, professional presentation.

  • Process: Describe the development process of your project. What was your MVP? What additional features did you add after fulfilling the requirements of your MVP? What features do you still hope to include?

  • Challenges: Share the biggest challenges you faced, and how you tackled them.

Additionally, allow time for any questions your audience may have.

Submissionâ€‹

Submit the link to your project's GitHub repository to the Independent Capstone Project code review on Epicenter before the end of the day of your presentation.

Visit Independent Projects and Code Reviews for details on how to submit your code, how feedback works and course completion requirements.
